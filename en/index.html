BMI Calculator

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measurement of body fat based on weight and height. However, BMI does not directly assess the amount of body fat. BMI calculators use an equation to arrive at an estimate. BMI calculation may assist a person in determining if they are at an unhealthy or healthy weight.

A high BMI typically indicates that there is too much fat on the body, while a low BMI might indicate that there is too little fat on the body. The greater the BMI score, the greater the chance of getting a medical condition such as diabetes, high blood pressure, or heart disease. An extremely low BMI, on the other hand, might lead to health issues such as anemia, impaired or diminished immunological function, and bone loss (osteoporosis).

How do you determine your BMI?

There is a simple method for folks who wish to determine their BMI. A person's Body Mass Index (BMI) may be determined by taking their weight in kilograms and dividing it by the square of their height in meters.

BMI = weight (in kgs)/height2

The World Health Organization (WHO) has issued the following recommendations for optimum BMI levels for adults (men and women over the age of 18):

• Underweight: less than 18.5
• Standard: 18.5 - 25
• Overweight: 25 - 30
• Obese: more than 30

However, the BMI value range varies from person to person and is influenced by variables such as heredity, ethnicity, gender, and a variety of other factors.

Most BMI calculators provide a weight chart to assist in interpreting the findings. While BMI is calculated the same manner for everyone, the findings of a BMI calculator are interpreted differently for children, teens, and adults.

What are the disadvantages of using a BMI Calculator?

Even though a BMI calculator is used to test adults and children for obesity, it has limitations. A BMI calculator has been discovered to often overestimate the amount of body fat in athletes and persons with particularly muscular physique. It has also been shown to underestimate the amount of body fat in those who have lost muscle mass as a result of a sickness or abrupt weight loss, as well as in elderly adults.

How reliable is a BMI calculator?

While a BMI calculator may be a good predictor of your health and fat levels, it is not 100 percent accurate in measuring your precise amount of body fat. Many medical professionals have previously challenged the use of BMI since it excludes a crucial component such as waist measurement, which is now regarded to be a key determining factor in obesity situations.
